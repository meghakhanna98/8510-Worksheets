Logansport Weekly Pharos May 30, 1883 pg. 5
Buffalo Bill and Dr. Carver will be here this summer with their Rocky mountain and prairie exhibition, known as The Wild West. The entertainment will be given at the fair grounds. One hundred and twenty people compromise the troupe, of which 60 are Indians, 6 Mexicans and 6 cowboys, Captain Carver, Col. Bogardus and son and Buffalo Bill. Sixty-five head of horses, 8 elks and 8 buffalo, including a baby buffalo, now two weeks old, born at Columbus, Nebraska, and called the "Nebraska Lilly." They have with them the celebrated Deadwood coach, in which Horace Greeley took his memorable ride. This coach will be drawn by six Mexican mules. There will be a street parade.
